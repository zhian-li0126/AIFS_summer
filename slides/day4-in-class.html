Why Light Matters

Light is the energy source for photosynthesis. In CEA, light is both a biological input and an engineering variable.

PAR, PPFD, and DLI

TermMeaningSimple interpretation
PARPhotosynthetically active radiation, roughly 400–700 nmLight plants can use for photosynthesis
PPFDPhotosynthetic photon flux densityHow many useful photons hit one square meter each second
DLIDaily light integralTotal useful light received in one day

PPFD Example

PPFD = μmol photons m⁻² s⁻¹

If two shelves have PPFD values of 150 and 300 μmol m⁻² s⁻¹, the second shelf receives twice as many photosynthetic photons per second.

DLI Calculation

DLI = PPFD × photoperiod × 3600 / 1,000,000

Example: PPFD = 250 μmol m⁻² s⁻¹ and photoperiod = 16 hours.

DLI = 250 × 16 × 3600 / 1,000,000 = 14.4 mol m⁻² day⁻¹.

Photoperiod

Photoperiod is how long lights are on each day.

Plants respond to both intensity and duration. A lower PPFD for longer time can sometimes provide a similar DLI as higher PPFD for shorter time.

200 PPFD for 18 hours gives DLI = 13.0 mol m⁻² day⁻¹.

300 PPFD for 12 hours gives DLI = 13.0 mol m⁻² day⁻¹.

Light Spectrum

Blue

Often affects compact growth and leaf morphology.

Red

Efficient for photosynthesis and growth.

Far-red / Green

Affects canopy penetration and plant signals.

Important: Plant responses depend on species, growth stage, intensity, and spectrum balance.

Light Use Efficiency

LUE = Plant biomass gained / Light energy or photons supplied

Light-use efficiency asks: how effectively did the plant convert supplied light into growth?

Lighting Uniformity

Uniformity means plants across a growing area receive similar light.

If one side of a tray receives 350 PPFD and the other receives 150 PPFD, plants may grow at different rates even if they are the same variety.

Lighting and Imaging Are Connected

The same plant can look different under different lighting conditions.

Brightness

Changes pixel intensity.

Spectrum

Changes color appearance.

Shadows

Change shape and texture features.

Example: Why Imaging Needs Controlled Light

Scenario: A model learns that yellow leaves indicate stress.

Problem: Under warm-colored lights, healthy leaves may appear more yellow.

Solution: Standardize imaging light or include lighting variation in training data.

Train / Validation / Test Split

Training

Used to learn model parameters.

Validation

Used to tune decisions during development.

Testing

Used once to estimate final performance.

Rule: Do not test on the same images used for training.

Data Augmentation

Augmentation creates modified versions of images to make the model more robust.

Flip

Left-right mirror.

Rotate

Slight angle changes.

Brightness

Simulate lighting changes.

Example Dataset Plan

Goal: Train a model to detect plant stress.

  • Collect images from multiple days.
  • Use consistent camera distance and angle.
  • Record lighting conditions.
  • Label each image as healthy, mild stress, or severe stress.
  • Keep a separate test set from plants or days not used in training.

Day 4 Key Takeaways

  • PPFD measures instantaneous photosynthetic light; DLI measures daily total light.
  • Spectrum, intensity, duration, and uniformity all matter.
  • Lighting strongly affects image appearance and model reliability.
  • Good image-training workflows need consistent data, labels, splits, and evaluation.
