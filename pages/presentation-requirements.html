AIFS Summer Camp

Presentation Requirements

For the final presentation, your team will propose an idea that uses machine learning, computer vision, IoT, or a combination of these tools for controlled environment agriculture.

Presentation Goal

The goal is not to show perfect results. Instead, your team should explain a thoughtful project idea and describe how you would approach the problem using the tools introduced during the camp.

Time Requirement

8 min Team Presentation
5 min Questions and Answers

What Your Team Should Include

  • Project idea: What problem or opportunity in greenhouse or indoor farming do you want to explore?
  • Motivation: Why does this problem matter for plants, growers, automation, or sustainability?
  • Data source: What kind of data would you need? Examples include images, sensor readings, light measurements, climate data, or nutrient data.
  • Method: What approach would you try? Examples include machine learning, computer vision, IoT sensing, sensor dashboards, object detection, classification, or simple control logic.
  • Expected workflow: What steps would your team follow from data collection to analysis?
  • Expected outcome: What would success look like, even if you do not have final results?
  • Challenges: What could make the project difficult? Examples include lighting changes, noisy sensors, limited data, camera placement, or labeling effort.

Suggested Presentation Structure

  1. Title and team members
  2. Problem statement
  3. Why the problem matters in CEA
  4. Data you would collect
  5. ML / CV / IoT approach you would use
  6. Expected workflow or system diagram
  7. Possible challenges and limitations
  8. Conclusion and next steps

Important Notes

  • No final experimental results are required.
  • Your team should focus on clear thinking, realistic methods, and a well-explained workflow.
  • Use diagrams, sketches, images, or example data if they help explain your idea.
  • Every team member should be ready to answer questions.
